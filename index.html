Section 2

The data visualization I want to talk about being particularly helpful and insightful are heat maps. Heat maps are a potent visualization tool, using color gradients to depict data density and concentration. Their intuitive design allows for quick identification of "hot spots" and trends, making them ideal for comparing data points and handling vast data sets. Heat maps proves their versatility through their constant utilizations across fields such as biology, finance, web design, and of course, geography. However, it is imperative to understand how easy it is to improperly manipulate heat maps such as choosing the wrong color scheme or using biased data. I think one of the most popular data visualization is often times misused and misinterpreted. That tool is the classic pie chart. I remember when in grade school, there would not be a poster I made in science class without a pie chart. Here is some examples on how they are flawed and misused. Flaw: They can be hard to interpret when there are many slices or if the differences in sizes of slices are subtle. Misuse: Representing more than 5-7 categories can make the chart confusing. Moreover, the use of 3D pie charts can distort the perceived sizes of the pie slices.
